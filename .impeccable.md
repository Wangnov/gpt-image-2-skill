## Design Context

### Users
GPT Image 2 serves creators and AI power users who repeatedly generate, edit, inspect, and deliver image assets across desktop, static web, and Docker-backed web runtimes. They are comfortable with model/provider settings, history, queues, and reference-image workflows, but still need the interface to stay fast enough for exploratory iteration.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel liquid, precise, and inventive: a design workbench for many visual styles rather than a single fixed skin. Motion should read as polished material behavior, not decoration for its own sake.

### Aesthetic Direction
The modern interface should prioritize refined liquid-glass motion, crystal-like image handling, and clear feedback for creative actions. Classic mode exists for users who prefer a quieter, lower-resource three-column workbench and should remain materially lighter than the modern interface. The app must respect reduced-motion preferences and avoid adding expensive movement where WebGL backgrounds already carry the visual load.

### Design Principles
- Motion should clarify state first, then add delight.
- Favor one coherent liquid-material language over scattered animation tricks.
- Keep high-frequency creation controls responsive and interruption-free.
- Preserve a strict performance budget: transform and opacity first, no layout-janky effects, and reduced-motion fallbacks everywhere.
- Modern mode can feel expressive; classic mode should stay efficient, predictable, and low-resource.
